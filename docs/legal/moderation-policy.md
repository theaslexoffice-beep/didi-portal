## 1. Purpose & Scope

### 1.1 Policy Objective
This Content Moderation Policy establishes clear guidelines for reviewing, approving, and rejecting citizen complaints submitted to the DIDI platform. Our goal is to maintain a safe, respectful, and effective grievance redressal system while protecting free expression and civic participation.

### 1.2 Applicable Content
This policy applies to all user-generated content, including:
- Complaint text and descriptions
- Media uploads (photos, videos, documents)
- Comments and updates
- User profiles and bios

### 1.3 Legal Framework
This policy is designed to comply with:
- **Article 19(1)(a)** of the Constitution of India (Freedom of Speech and Expression)
- **Article 19(2)** of the Constitution (Reasonable Restrictions)
- **Information Technology Act, 2000**, particularly Sections 66A (struck down), 67, 69A
- **Indian Penal Code, 1860** - Sections 153A (communal disharmony), 499-502 (defamation), 292-294 (obscenity)
- **Right to Information Act, 2005**

---

## 2. Moderation Principles

### 2.1 Core Values
Our moderation is guided by:
- **Transparency**: Clear, publicly accessible rules
- **Consistency**: Uniform application across all complaints
- **Proportionality**: Minimum necessary intervention
- **Due Process**: Fair review with appeal rights
- **Context-Sensitivity**: Cultural and linguistic nuances considered

### 2.2 Presumption of Legitimacy
We presume all complaints are made in good faith unless evidence suggests otherwise. The burden is on moderators to justify rejection.

### 2.3 Human Review
**All moderation decisions are made by trained human moderators**, not automated systems alone. AI may flag content for review but never makes final decisions.

---

## 3. Moderation Process Flow

### 3.1 Submission Stage
**User Action:** Submit complaint via WhatsApp/web interface

**System Action:**
- Assign unique Complaint ID
- Send acknowledgment to user
- Set status to "Pending Review"
- Queue for moderation (FIFO basis)

### 3.2 Review Stage
**Moderator Review (within 48 hours):**
1. Check for prohibited content (Section 5)
2. Verify complaint relevance and jurisdiction
3. Assess supporting evidence
4. Apply moderation decision matrix

**Possible Outcomes:**
- ✅ **Approve**: Publish complaint
- ⚠️ **Request Clarification**: Ask user for additional information
- ❌ **Reject**: Deny publication with reason
- 🔍 **Escalate**: Refer to senior moderator or legal team

### 3.3 Post-Decision Stage
**If Approved:**
- Complaint published on platform
- User notified via WhatsApp
- Relevant authority identified and notified
- Status updated to "Active"

**If Rejected:**
- User notified with specific reason
- Appeal instructions provided
- Complaint remains in "Rejected" status (not deleted)

**If Clarification Requested:**
- User given 7 days to respond
- Auto-reject after 7 days if no response
- Can be resubmitted after clarification

## 5. Prohibited Content

### 5.1 Automatic Rejection Categories

#### A. Hate Speech & Communal Incitement
**Definition:** Content that promotes hatred, violence, or discrimination based on:
- Religion, caste, community, ethnicity
- Gender, sexual orientation
- Regional identity, language
- Disability or health condition

**Examples of Prohibited Content:**
- ❌ "All [community] members are criminals"
- ❌ "We should drive [group] out of our area"
- ❌ Symbols or slogans associated with banned organizations
- ❌ Calls for boycott based on identity

**Exceptions:**
- ✅ Reporting hate crimes or discrimination (factual documentation)
- ✅ Quoting prohibited speech as evidence

#### B. Defamation & Personal Attacks
**Definition:** False statements that harm reputation or dignity of identifiable individuals.

**Prohibited:**
- ❌ Unsubstantiated accusations of criminal conduct
- ❌ "Name-and-shame" attacks on specific officials without evidence
- ❌ Publishing private information to cause harm (doxxing)
- ❌ Morphed or doctored images to defame

**Permitted:**
- ✅ Criticism of official conduct with supporting evidence
- ✅ Statements of opinion clearly marked as such
- ✅ Matters of public interest (proportionate to evidence)

#### C. Obscenity & Sexual Content
**Prohibited:**
- ❌ Pornographic or sexually explicit imagery
- ❌ Non-consensual intimate images
- ❌ Sexualized content involving minors
- ❌ Graphic sexual descriptions

**Permitted:**
- ✅ Medical or educational content (clinical context)
- ✅ Evidence of sexual harassment (appropriately censored)

#### D. Violence & Threats
**Prohibited:**
- ❌ Direct threats of violence against any person
- ❌ Graphic violence without content warning
- ❌ Instructions for harmful activities
- ❌ Glorification of violence

**Permitted:**
- ✅ Documentation of violence as evidence (with warnings)
- ✅ Descriptions necessary for grievance context

#### E. False Information & Manipulation
**Prohibited:**
- ❌ Deliberately fabricated complaints
- ❌ Deepfakes or misleading doctored media
- ❌ Impersonation of officials or authorities
- ❌ Coordinated inauthentic behavior (fake campaigns)

**Permitted:**
- ✅ Honest mistakes or misunderstandings (correctable)
- ✅ Opinions or speculation clearly labeled as such

#### F. Spam & Abuse of Platform
**Prohibited:**
- ❌ Duplicate submissions of same complaint
- ❌ Mass submission of unrelated grievances
- ❌ Commercial advertising or solicitation
- ❌ Malicious use to harass officials with frivolous complaints

#### G. Out-of-Scope Content
**Rejected (not prohibited, but inappropriate for platform):**
- ⚠️ Private disputes between individuals (not public grievances)
- ⚠️ Complaints outside Bilaspur/Chhattisgarh jurisdiction
- ⚠️ Matters pending in court (sub judice)
- ⚠️ Requests for personal favors or benefits

## 6. Approval Criteria

### 6.1 Complaints Should Be Approved If:
✅ **Genuine Public Grievance**: Relates to government services, infrastructure, or public interest  
✅ **Relevant Jurisdiction**: Falls within Bilaspur/Chhattisgarh or central government matters  
✅ **Sufficiently Detailed**: Provides enough information for authorities to act  
✅ **Respectful Tone**: May be critical but not abusive  
✅ **Supported by Evidence**: Photos, documents, or credible description  
✅ **Clear Identity**: Verifiable complainant (even if published anonymously)  

### 6.2 Marginal Cases - Moderator Discretion
For borderline content, moderators should:
1. **Err on the side of approval** if content serves public interest
2. Consider context (emotional expression vs. deliberate hate speech)
3. Offer editorial guidance (e.g., "Consider rephrasing this section")
4. Seek second opinion from senior moderator

---

## 7. Appeal Process

### 7.1 Right to Appeal
Any user whose complaint is rejected may appeal the decision within **14 days** of notification.

### 7.2 Appeal Procedure
**Step 1: File Appeal**
- Email to appeals@didi.org.in
- Include: Complaint ID, reason for appeal, additional evidence (if any)

**Step 2: Independent Review**
- Reviewed by different moderator (not the original reviewer)
- Senior moderator involved for complex cases
- Legal team consulted if needed

**Step 3: Decision**
- Appeal decision within 5 working days
- Options: Overturn (approve complaint), Uphold (maintain rejection), Conditional approval (with edits)

**Step 4: Final Recourse**
- If appeal denied, user may resubmit with modifications addressing the concerns
- Escalate to DIDI Board of Directors (for policy disputes)

### 7.3 Appeal Transparency
Quarterly reports published on:
- Total appeals filed
- Overturn rate by rejection category
- Common reasons for successful appeals

---

## 8. Admin Discretion & Oversight

### 8.1 Moderator Authority
Moderators have discretion to:
- Request additional evidence before approval
- Suggest edits for clarity or compliance
- Apply content warnings to sensitive material
- Delay publication for legal review (rare, justified cases)

### 8.2 Limitations on Discretion
Moderators **may not**:
- Reject content based on political viewpoint
- Suppress criticism of government or officials
- Favor complaints from specific groups
- Make decisions based on external pressure

### 8.3 Accountability Mechanisms
- **Moderation Log**: All decisions recorded with justification
- **Audit Trail**: Quarterly internal audit of moderation decisions
- **Bias Training**: Regular unconscious bias and free speech training
- **Grievance Redressal**: Users can report moderation abuse to DIDI leadership

### 8.4 Emergency Moderation
In rare cases of imminent harm or legal emergency:
- Content may be temporarily removed pending review
- User notified within 24 hours
- Review completed within 72 hours
- Requires approval from two senior moderators

---

## 9. Post-Publication Moderation

### 9.1 User Reporting
Published complaints can be flagged by:
- Platform users
- Authorities mentioned in complaint
- Third parties claiming harm

### 9.2 Review of Published Content
Flagged content is reviewed using the same standards as initial moderation.

**Possible Actions:**
- **No Action**: Flag deemed unsubstantiated
- **Content Warning**: Add label for sensitive content
- **Redaction**: Remove specific details (e.g., personal contact info)
- **Takedown**: Remove entirely (rare, requires strong justification)

### 9.3 Takedown Process
Content may be removed post-publication if:
- Court order received
- False information proven (malicious intent)
- User requests deletion (Right to be Forgotten)
- Legal threat to platform (proportionate response)

**Transparency:** Takedown notices published (redacted for privacy) explaining reason and legal basis.

---

## 10. Training & Quality Assurance

### 10.1 Moderator Training
All moderators complete training in:
- Constitutional principles (Article 19)
- Indian defamation and hate speech law
- Platform policies and procedures
- De-escalation and communication
- Cultural sensitivity and regional context

### 10.2 Quality Metrics
Moderation quality measured by:
- **Consistency**: Inter-moderator agreement rate (target: >85%)
- **Timeliness**: % of reviews within 48 hours (target: >95%)
- **Appeal Overturn Rate**: (target: <15% - indicates accurate first review)
- **User Satisfaction**: Post-resolution surveys

### 10.3 Continuous Improvement
- Monthly moderator calibration sessions
- Case study discussions of difficult decisions
- Policy updates based on learnings

---

## 11. Transparency & Public Reporting

### 11.1 Quarterly Transparency Report
DIDI publishes data on:
- Total complaints submitted
- Approval vs. rejection rates by category
- Average review time
- Appeal statistics
- Moderator performance metrics

### 11.2 Policy Updates
Any changes to this policy will be:
- Published 30 days before implementation
- Announced via WhatsApp/email
- Subject to public comment period
- Approved by DIDI Board

### 1.1 नीति का उद्देश्य
यह सामग्री मॉडरेशन नीति DIDI मंच पर प्रस्तुत नागरिक शिकायतों की समीक्षा, अनुमोदन और अस्वीकार के लिए स्पष्ट दिशानिर्देश स्थापित करती है। हमारा लक्ष्य एक सुरक्षित, सम्मानजनक और प्रभावी शिकायत निवारण प्रणाली बनाए रखना है।

## 2. मॉडरेशन प्रक्रिया

### 2.1 प्रस्तुति चरण
**उपयोगकर्ता कार्रवाई:** व्हाट्सएप/वेब इंटरफ़ेस के माध्यम से शिकायत प्रस्तुत करें

**सिस्टम कार्रवाई:**
- अनूठा शिकायत आईडी असाइन करें
- उपयोगकर्ता को स्वीकृति भेजें
- स्थिति को "समीक्षा लंबित" पर सेट करें
- मॉडरेशन के लिए कतार (FIFO आधार)

### 2.2 समीक्षा चरण
**मॉडरेटर समीक्षा (48 घंटे के भीतर):**
1. निषिद्ध सामग्री के लिए जांचें
2. शिकायत की प्रासंगिकता और क्षेत्राधिकार सत्यापित करें
3. सहायक साक्ष्य का आकलन करें
4. मॉडरेशन निर्णय मैट्रिक्स लागू करें

**संभावित परिणाम:**
- ✅ **अनुमोदित करें**: शिकायत प्रकाशित करें
- ⚠️ **स्पष्टीकरण का अनुरोध करें**: अतिरिक्त जानकारी के लिए पूछें
- ❌ **अस्वीकार करें**: कारण के साथ प्रकाशन को अस्वीकार करें

## 4. निषिद्ध सामग्री

### 4.1 स्वचालित अस्वीकृति श्रेणियां

#### A. घृणा भाषण और सांप्रदायिक उकसावा
**परिभाषा:** ऐसी सामग्री जो इसके आधार पर घृणा, हिंसा या भेदभाव को बढ़ावा देती है:
- धर्म, जाति, समुदाय, जातीयता
- लिंग, यौन अभिविन्यास
- क्षेत्रीय पहचान, भाषा

**निषिद्ध सामग्री के उदाहरण:**
- ❌ "सभी [समुदाय] के सदस्य अपराधी हैं"
- ❌ "हमें [समूह] को हमारे क्षेत्र से बाहर निकालना चाहिए"
- ❌ पहचान के आधार पर बहिष्कार के लिए कॉल

#### B. मानहानि और व्यक्तिगत हमले
**परिभाषा:** झूठे बयान जो पहचाने जाने योग्य व्यक्तियों की प्रतिष्ठा या गरिमा को हानि पहुंचाते हैं।

**निषिद्ध:**
- ❌ आपराधिक आचरण के निराधार आरोप
- ❌ साक्ष्य के बिना विशिष्ट अधिकारियों पर "नाम-और-शर्म" हमले
- ❌ हानि पहुंचाने के लिए निजी जानकारी प्रकाशित करना

**अनुमत:**
- ✅ सहायक साक्ष्य के साथ आधिकारिक आचरण की आलोचना
- ✅ जनहित के मामले (साक्ष्य के अनुपात में)

#### C. अश्लीलता और यौन सामग्री
**निषिद्ध:**
- ❌ अश्लील या यौन रूप से स्पष्ट छवियां
- ❌ गैर-सहमति वाली अंतरंग छवियां
- ❌ नाबालिगों से जुड़ी यौन सामग्री

#### D. हिंसा और धमकियां
**निषिद्ध:**
- ❌ किसी भी व्यक्ति के खिलाफ हिंसा की प्रत्यक्ष धमकियां
- ❌ बिना सामग्री चेतावनी के ग्राफिक हिंसा
- ❌ हानिकारक गतिविधियों के निर्देश

#### E. झूठी जानकारी और हेरफेर
**निषिद्ध:**
- ❌ जानबूझकर बनाई गई शिकायतें
- ❌ डीपफेक या भ्रामक डॉक्टर्ड मीडिया
- ❌ अधिकारियों या अधिकारियों का प्रतिरूपण

#### F. स्पैम और मंच का दुरुपयोग
**निषिद्ध:**
- ❌ समान शिकायत की डुप्लिकेट सबमिशन
- ❌ असंबंधित शिकायतों का बड़े पैमाने पर सबमिशन
- ❌ व्यावसायिक विज्ञापन या याचना

## 5. अनुमोदन मानदंड

### 5.1 शिकायतों को अनुमोदित किया जाना चाहिए यदि:
✅ **वास्तविक सार्वजनिक शिकायत**: सरकारी सेवाओं, बुनियादी ढांचे या जनहित से संबंधित  
✅ **प्रासंगिक क्षेत्राधिकार**: बिलासपुर/छत्तीसगढ़ या केंद्र सरकार के मामलों के अंतर्गत आता है  
✅ **पर्याप्त विस्तृत**: अधिकारियों के कार्य करने के लिए पर्याप्त जानकारी प्रदान करता है  
✅ **सम्मानजनक स्वर**: आलोचनात्मक हो सकता है लेकिन अपमानजनक नहीं  
✅ **साक्ष्य द्वारा समर्थित**: फोटो, दस्तावेज़ या विश्वसनीय विवरण  
✅ **स्पष्ट पहचान**: सत्यापन योग्य शिकायतकर्ता
